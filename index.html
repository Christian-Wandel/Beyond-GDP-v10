GNSD — Gross National Sustainable Development

What it measures. A single apex score (0–100) that combines economic output with multidimensional living standards. It answers: is growth translating into better lives within planetary limits?

Formula (UNEN Working Paper No. 1, Samans et al., March 2026):

GNSD = 0.4 × GDP per capita (normalized) + 0.6 × MLSI pillar average

GDP weighted at 40%: economic output is a means to an end. Human living standards (MLSI) weighted at 60% as the primary measure of national development.

The five MLSI pillars (each 0–100):

  • O — Opportunity: labour underutilization, NEET rate, mean years of schooling
  • I — Income: average wage (PPP), labour productivity, poverty rate
  • N — Necessities: multidimensional poverty, material footprint
  • EcS — Economic & Social Security: healthy life expectancy, UHC coverage
  • EnS — Environmental Security: CO₂ per capita, ecological footprint vs biocapacity

Normalization: UNDP goalpost method. GDP per capita floor $1,000, ceiling $80,000 (log scale). Each MLSI indicator normalized 0–100 against fixed absolute goalposts.

Ecological floor cap: A country scoring <40 on Environmental Security cannot score above 60 overall — ecological overshoot is treated as a non-tradeable constraint.

Reading the score: ≥70 green (on track), 40–69 amber (mixed), <40 red (lagging). Tick marks on the bar sit at 40 and 70.
